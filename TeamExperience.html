Lebogane Mpakati - Professional history

Lebogane resumed her career in business rescue, turnarounds, liquidations and insolvencies from the experience she gained in Turnarounds, Workouts and Restructuring at the Industrial Development Corporation where she was employed as a Senior Account Manager from 2005. It was here where she carried out feasibility studies on IDC’s clients that were in distress. She was responsible for determining whether the IDC’s clients had the potential to become economically viable again by implementing the most feasible solution to prevent the client’s financial failure. She has extensive experience over a broad range of industries such as mining, construction, textile, manufacturing and the service industry.

Zolani Buba - Professional history

Zolani worked at SARS from May 2014 to October 2018, where he managed business rescue matters within SARS at national level spanning across sectors and multiple industries.

From January 2014 to July 2015, Zolani was part of Davis Tax Committee BEPS (Base Erosion and Profit Shifting) Sub-Committee where his role included the Submission of Technical Reports on the OECD Action Plan with a particular focus on the Prevention of Artificial Avoidance of a Permanent Establishment, Treaty Abuse as well as the allocation of risk and capital by Multinational Enterprises.

He also engaged with Sub-Committee Chair’s on various reports submitted inclusive re-submission where necessary.

Prior to joining SARS, Zolani worked for Ernst & Young where his functions included the provision of legal opinions on South African Corporate Tax and VAT implications on proposed transactions for Ernst & Young clients.

Lesetja Freddy Mohlaka - Professional history

Freddy’s has built an extensive career in the legal fraternity and boasts over 15 years experience. Amongst other multinational corporations, Freddy has worked with Standard Bank, Coco-cola and the Department of Enterprises South Africa.

Freddy has extensive experience in corporate legal advisory and is sought after for his in-depth knowledge in commercial and company law. As an advisor to the Chief Director in Ministry of Public Enterprises, Freddy was responsible for the transformation and restructuring of R70 billion in various state entities such as SAA, ACSA, Telkom, Transnet and PETRO SA. To date, he continues to provide legal support to the Ministry and Parliament’s committees and occupies various boards.
