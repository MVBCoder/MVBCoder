  • article2020/07/06 - Building your Digital Marketing strategy: Why you should be all in during the Covid19 Pandemic.

    Now, more than ever, business across all sectors are living through unprecedent times. The COVID-19 pandemic has drastically changed the way in which we effectively engage our consumer market and reach our clientele. Whether its conducting a meeting or building new relations, there is little room for entrepreneurs to escape the reality of digitalization and the coming of the 4th industrial revolution.

    The effects of the pandemic will be felt long after its left and your business must be ready to restrategize and adapt. Strong digital marketing strategies are fast causing major disruptions and setting forward thinkers ahead of the curve. Setting in motion a digital strategy that will benefit your business for the future is not only smart, its vital.

    So before you shrug off the idea, here are some things to think about.

    1. You Need to Adapt

    Businesses that rely on human interaction or that depend on foot traffic have without a doubt taken the hardest hit on their profits over the past few months. The regulations around COVID-19 have significantly reduced the amount of traffic they would ordinarily get which means these business owners must now find new and innovative ways of gaining exposure.

    While social distancing may have created physical distance between us, it’s connected businesses and consumers in whole new ways. People are now spending more time online and getting comfortable with connecting with different brand personae. This is why digital forms of communication are so essential. It allows you to reach your customers at the right place and at the right time while giving your business gets the opportunity to stay visible and heard without being physically present.

    2. Reinforce brand identity and brand loyalty

    If anything, the pandemic has made people more weary and cautious about what to think and believe. Restricted access to products and pay cuts have made us feel like we could possibly “do without” or find a cheaper alternative.

    A focused digital marketing strategy gives you the opportunity to let your consumer base know that you still know and understand their needs. People want to know that their favorite brands care about what’s happening and most importantly that they are taking the necessary measures to find creative ways to still deliver. Through online tools like newsletters, instant messages and web automated chat, your customers can have access to you in real time and constantly be updated on all your new service offerings and changes.

    3. Its measurable and personalized.

    Personalization. A word that has quickly become synonymous with the digital era. As your customers options increase, so does their inability to make a decision. An online presence gives you the opportunity to decide for the undecided market by personalizing your messaging to appeal to their needs and wants. This evidently means more customers for you and your business.

    Online marketing gives you the freedom to personalize your messaging at leaves room for the flexibility to change your message to what the climate dictates. While traditional forms of advertising are rigid and appeal to a strict demographic, online data can tell you what you need to know about your client before sending your messaging out. What’s more is platforms such as Facebook, Instagram and paid promotional posts are all accompanied by easy to read KPI’s so that you can effectively monitor your reach and tweak your strategy accordingly.

    Conclusion

    A digital marketing strategy will help you develop your business goals by identifying the needs of your potential customer and how to best meet them. More than this, it will help your business stay relevant through the ever-changing trends and get you closer to your customers.
